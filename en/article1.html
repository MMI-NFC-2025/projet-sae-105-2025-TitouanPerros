HER PHOTOGRAPHIC APPROACH

Lucia Moholy documented the Bauhaus and the work of its artists with a precise and innovative eye. Her photography combines documentary rigor and aesthetic sense, revealing the beauty of forms, lines and textures.

OBJECTIVE PHOTOGRAPHY

She practiced an approach called objective photography: each object or work is photographed neutrally, but with an emphasis on light, composition and perspectives, making it possible to transform the everyday into graphic and modern images.

VISUAL EXPERIMENTATION

Inspired by the experimental spirit of the Bauhaus, Lucia Moholy explored new angles, shadows, reflections, and played with light to create photographs that were both documentary and artistic.

HER ROLE IN SPREADING THE BAUHAUS

Thanks to her photographs, the works of the Bauhaus were documented and disseminated throughout the world. Her work helps to understand the vision of artists while highlighting the innovation and modernity of this school.

A SCIENTIFIC PHOTOGRAPHER?

Lucia Moholy is not considered a woman scientist as such. However, she had a very particular approach to photography.
Her photos possessed many scientific characteristics (methodological rigor, use of technique). She was an artist with strong technical mastery who implemented good intellectual approaches through her works.

This exhibition at Kunsthalle Praha pays tribute to Lucia Moholy, a pioneering Bauhaus photographer. Through archives and previously unpublished research, the project explores her entire career, from her famous photographs of modern architecture to her portraits in exile, aiming to rehabilitate her major and often overlooked role in the history of 20th-century photography.
