GLOSSARY

Nuclear fission: Nuclear fission is a reaction in which the nucleus of a heavy atom splits into two lighter nuclei, releasing a large amount of energy as well as neutrons.
Kepler's tables: Kepler's tables are astronomical tables developed by the scholar Johannes Kepler (in the 17th century) to predict the position of planets in the sky at different times.
Bougainville's expedition: This was the first official circumnavigation of the world by a Frenchman. Bougainville departed with two ships, La Boudeuse and L'Étoile, to explore and map new lands in the Pacific.
An algorithm: An algorithm is a sequence of instructions or logical steps that allows solving a problem or performing a specific task.
A mentorship program: A mentorship program is a structured support system that connects an experienced person (the mentor) with a person in learning or development (the mentee).
